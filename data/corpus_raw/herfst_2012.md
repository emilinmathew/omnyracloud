---
title: "Herfst et al. 2012: Airborne transmission of influenza A/H5N1 via mutant viruses"
source: "Science 336(6088), 2012"
---

Herfst et al. independently demonstrated airborne transmission of H5N1 in ferrets using a different approach: ten rounds of serial passage in ferrets, selecting for enhanced adaptation to the mammalian respiratory tract. The passaged virus acquired five mutations total, two in hemagglutinin and one each in the polymerase complex subunits. Key mutations included changes at HA positions 138, 225, and 226 (receptor binding site), and PB2-627K. The methods section described: intranasal inoculation of ferrets with 10^6 TCID50 of H5N1, harvest of nasal washes at day 3 post-infection, centrifugation to remove cell debris, titration by plaque assay on MDCK cells, and passage into the next ferret. Transmissibility was confirmed by placing naive ferrets in adjacent cages with airflow directed from infected to naive ferrets. Both this paper and the Fouchier paper triggered the first pandemic pathogen research (PPR) moratorium and established the current NSABB/P3CO review framework for gain-of-function research.
