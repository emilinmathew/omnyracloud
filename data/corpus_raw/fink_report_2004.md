---
title: "Fink Report 2004: Seven experiments of concern"
source: "National Academies Press, 'Biotechnology Research in an Age of Terrorism', 2004"
---

The 2004 Fink Report (formally 'Biotechnology Research in an Age of Terrorism') defined seven categories of experiments that warrant special oversight due to their potential for misuse. These are the foundational taxonomy for biosecurity concern evaluation. The seven categories are:

1. Demonstrate methods for rendering vaccines ineffective. (Maps to: resistance_immune_evasion)
2. Confer resistance to therapeutics. (Maps to: resistance_immune_evasion)
3. Enhance virulence of a pathogen or render it more toxic. (Maps to: enhanced_transmissibility)
4. Increase transmissibility of a pathogen. (Maps to: enhanced_transmissibility)
5. Alter the host range of a pathogen. (Maps to: enhanced_transmissibility, delivery_system_construction)
6. Enable evasion of detection and diagnosis. (Maps to: sequence_screening_evasion)
7. Enable weaponization of a biological agent or toxin. (Maps to: delivery_system_construction, toxin_production)

The report recommended a dual-use research oversight committee (now NSABB) and contributed to the creation of the NIH recombinant DNA advisory committee review process for DURC. The categories remain the normative framework for evaluating dual-use research of concern and map directly to the six threat taxonomy categories used in this screening system.
