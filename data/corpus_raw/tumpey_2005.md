---
title: "Tumpey et al. 2005: Characterization of the reconstructed 1918 influenza pandemic virus"
source: "Science 310(5745), 2005"
---

Tumpey et al. at the CDC reconstructed the 1918 Spanish influenza virus using reverse genetics from sequences recovered from archived autopsy material and a preserved Alaskan permafrost sample. The protocol involved cloning all eight gene segments of the 1918 H1N1 virus into reverse genetics plasmids, transfecting into MDCK/293T cells using the Hoffmann et al. plasmid-based reverse genetics system, and recovering infectious 1918 virus. The reconstructed virus was studied under BSL-4 conditions at the CDC. The paper established the genetic basis for the exceptional virulence of the 1918 pandemic and is a foundational example of select agent reconstruction via reverse genetics. All eight cDNA segments of the 1918 influenza virus are listed as controlled sequences. The protocol type (assembly of viral genome segments via reverse genetics, transfection, virus recovery) is a canonical indicator for the select_agent_synthesis taxonomy category.
