---
title: "IGSC Harmonized Screening Guidelines"
source: "International Gene Synthesis Consortium (IGSC), 2023 Harmonized Screening Protocol"
---

The International Gene Synthesis Consortium (IGSC) Harmonized Screening Protocol requires member companies to screen all customer-submitted gene sequences and orders against a database of sequences from regulated pathogens and toxin genes. The protocol operates at the sequence level: each ordered sequence is aligned against sequences from CDC/USDA select agents, toxin genes regulated under the Select Agent Regulations, and sequences from pathogens with pandemic potential. Orders containing sequences with significant homology (typically >85% identity over a defined window) to regulated sequences are flagged for review. The IGSC protocol includes a two-tier screen: (1) sequence homology check against a curated database, and (2) order-level review including customer information and synthesis context. The critical limitation of sequence-level screening is that it cannot detect (a) protocols that pursue dangerous objectives without any controlled DNA synthesis, (b) workflows that split a controlled sequence across multiple orders, (c) protocols that use functional analogs with low homology, or (d) workflows using existing biological materials. Protocol-level screening is the complementary layer needed to address these gaps.
