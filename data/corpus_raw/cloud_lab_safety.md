---
title: "Cloud Lab Platforms: Safety and Biosecurity Considerations"
source: "Autoprotocol.org specification; Strateos platform documentation; Emerald Cloud Lab safety overview"
---

Cloud laboratory platforms (Strateos, Emerald Cloud Lab, Transcriptic) enable remote execution of biological experiments through structured protocol languages. Autoprotocol is the primary open standard; it describes experiments as sequences of typed instructions operating on reagents stored in defined containers. The cloud lab execution model presents a specific biosecurity challenge: (1) the lab operator executing the protocol may not understand the biological objective, (2) reagents can be provisioned from inventory without explicit researcher synthesis requests, (3) protocols can be composed programmatically, enabling systematic splitting of concerning workflows, and (4) execution logs may not map cleanly to biological outcomes. Current cloud lab safety practices focus on reagent inventory control, BSL compliance for facility designation, and operator training. None of the major cloud lab platforms currently perform protocol-level biosecurity screening — their safety review is focused on reagent hazard classification and physical safety rather than dual-use concern detection. The workflow-level screening gap is the primary motivation for protocol-level biosecurity tools.
